## 3. Major new verified findings

### 3.1 Elizabeth Gill primary text

A free full 1738 edition was located in Google Play Books:

- John Gill, *A Sermon occasioned by the Death of Elizabeth Gill... To which is added, an Account of some of her Choice Experiences*;
- ESTC T67704;
- Folger record and ECCO/British Library provenance;
- 54 digital pages / 44 printed pages in the Folger description.

Editorial consequence:

- the `Choice Experiences` section must not automatically be called an independent diary or autobiography by Elizabeth;
- the printed item is Gill’s funeral sermon plus an edited family account including reported words and experiences;
- the article must mark the genre and narrator.

### 3.2 Archival route identified

The Particular Baptist Fund description identifies letters addressed to Gill at folios 9, 11, 19 and 22, plus Carter Lane network correspondence.

The Angus Library and Archive is confirmed as the principal Baptist archive with church records, minute books and manuscript letters, and the National Archives index lists John Gill as an archival subject.

Access constraint:

- Angus is officially closed to visitors and enquiries from January through October 2026;
- the Gill enquiry packet should be prepared now and submitted after reopening.

### 3.3 Spurgeon primary evaluation

Spurgeon’s *Commenting and Commentaries* was checked through a public-domain facsimile record and full CCEL text.

Verified positive claims:

- `A very distinguished place is due to Dr. Gill`;
- exceptional Hebrew and rabbinic learning;
- sound, massive and sober sense;
- continued value of the commentary.

Verified critical claims:

- `his ultraism is discarded`;
- the system is described as rigid;
- Gill may force difficult texts into systematic shape;
- `Gill is the Coryphaeus of hyper-Calvinism`.

Correction:

- the common statement that Spurgeon assigned Gill `three stars` has not been verified in the 1876 facsimile;
- Spurgeon’s stated recommendation system is typographic size and italics, so a star rating may be a later editorial representation.

### 3.4 Park/Strother/Ascol/Walden access states

- Park 2001: old Aberdeen ID `AAIU147951` and `Full text` indexing survive, but no current official bitstream has been obtained.
- Strother 2023: dissertation is bibliographically confirmed by Themelios; no public repository PDF was found.
- Ascol 1989: substantial page-numbered excerpts are online, but the dissertation remains unavailable; excerpts cannot replace full context.
- Walden 2024: official Wiley record confirms pp. 176–196 and the abstract; direct PDF returned 403.

Editorial correction:

The site currently treats Walden as an accomplished `academic rehabilitation` that makes Gill compatible with Reformed orthodoxy and proves prior scholarship wrong. The abstract supports a more precise defensive reconstruction, not a closed scholarly consensus. The future site text must describe Walden as one significant contemporary argument in a continuing debate.

### 3.5 Publication and edition control

PRDL records 113 titles and 148 volumes. The new chronology distinguishes:

- oral delivery;
- first printed issue;
- completion of serial/multivolume works;
- revised or collected edition;
- posthumous publication.

Critical date conflicts registered:

- Old Testament Exposition: 1763 vs 1766;
- Doctrinal Divinity: 1767 vs 1769;
- Eternal Sonship: composition vs posthumous publication;
- Cause: 1735–38 parts vs later complete editions;
- collected sermons: 1773 vols. I–II and catalogued 1778 vol. III.

Rule:

A page from Tegg 1838 or the 1809–1810 editions must not be cited as though it were the pagination of a 1730s first issue.

## 4. Source-quality changes

The research now uses explicit access classes:

- A1 — facsimile/official full primary PDF;
- A2 — stable full machine text;
- A3 — official bibliographic record only;
- B1/B3 — full academic PDF/journal archive;
- B2 — abstract/preview only;
- C1/C2 — transcription or unofficial mirror for navigation;
- X — source or page not yet obtained.

This prevents three recurring failures:

1. citing a catalogue record as if the book was read;
2. citing an unofficial transcript as the source of record;
3. presenting an abstract’s thesis as a page-verified conclusion.

## 5. Current editorial conclusions

1. Gill affirmed broad public proclamation and a real external call.
2. This does not establish Fullerian duty-faith or a universal well-meant offer.
3. Saving faith, general responsibility, natural duty, external call and effectual calling must remain distinct.
4. Sandeman is not a Gill disciple; Glas/Sandeman forms a separate genealogy.
5. Gill–Whitefield evidence remains mixed and source-dependent.
6. Eternal justification requires immanent, representative, accomplished, applied and manifested dimensions.
7. Gill’s Trinity, Christology, Hebrew studies and practical divinity are central, not decorative side topics.
8. Spurgeon is a critical heir: institutional and Calvinistic continuity, explicit rejection of Gill’s ultraism.
9. Institutional continuity to Metropolitan Tabernacle does not establish complete theological identity.
10. Claims that Gill caused Baptist decline or pioneered the whole Awakening remain historiographical arguments, not established single-cause facts.
